# Day 5 — AI App Design

**Date:** Monday, May 18, 2026  
**Goal:** By the end of today, interns will understand chatbot anatomy and design a chatbot concept for a real user.

## Kickoff (0:00 - 0:10)

**Warm-up:** "What is a boring or confusing task a chatbot could help with?"

**Recap:** Last session we learned responsible AI use. Today we start thinking like builders.

**Today's goal:** "Design a chatbot with a clear audience, personality, job, and limits."

## Learn Block (0:10 - 0:50)

### Concepts
- AI app = AI + user interface + purpose
- Chatbot anatomy:
  - User goal
  - System instructions
  - Personality
  - Knowledge
  - Edge cases
- Good bots have boundaries: what they do, what they refuse, when they ask for clarification
- Design before building saves time

### Live Demo
Compare:

> "You are a study bot."

with:

> "You are StudySprint, a friendly study coach for high school students. Help users break homework into 25-minute focus blocks. Ask one question at a time. Do not give medical or mental health advice."

### Pause Points
- "Who is this bot for?"
- "What should the bot refuse to do?"
- "What would make the bot feel useful instead of generic?"

## Build Block (1:00 - 1:50)

### Project: Chatbot Blueprint

Design your chatbot on paper or in a doc:

- Bot name
- Target user
- Problem it solves
- Personality in 3 words
- Opening message
- System prompt draft
- 5 questions users might ask
- 3 edge cases and how the bot should respond

**Stretch:** Add a small knowledge base: 5 facts, rules, or resources the bot should use.

## Play Block (2:00 - 2:40)

### Activity: Chatbot Roast

Pairs swap chatbot blueprints and try to find:

- Confusing purpose
- Missing user
- Unsafe advice
- Too much personality
- Not enough personality

Keep it constructive: every roast must include one fix.

## Share & Wrap (2:40 - 3:00)

**Share prompt:** "Pitch your chatbot in 30 seconds: user, problem, personality."

**Next-session preview:** "Tomorrow your blueprint becomes a working chatbot prototype."

## Post-Session Notes

- Check that every blueprint is buildable in one session.
- Save strong system prompts as examples for Day 11 capstone prompts.
