# Day 4 — Responsible AI + Mini-Showcase

**Date:** Friday, May 15, 2026  
**Goal:** By the end of today, interns will understand core AI risks and will package their first practice artifacts into a short mini-showcase.

## Kickoff (0:00 - 0:10)

**Warm-up:** "When should someone not trust an AI answer?"

**Recap:** This week we explored AI, improved prompts, and created writing and images. Today we learn how to use AI responsibly and show what we made.

**Today's goal:** "Understand AI's limits, create a responsible-use reflection, and share your strongest practice artifact."

## Learn Block (0:10 - 0:50)

### Concepts
- Hallucinations: confident answers can be wrong
- Bias: AI can repeat patterns from unfair data
- Privacy: do not paste secrets, addresses, passwords, or private messages
- Attribution: say when AI helped
- Verification: check important claims before using them
- Human responsibility: the final choice is yours

### Demo: AI Fact Check
Ask AI:

> "Give me 5 facts about a current technology trend."

Then pick one claim and verify it with a trusted source or by asking for uncertainty and citations.

### Pause Points
- "Which claims would you verify before sharing?"
- "What personal information should never go into a prompt?"
- "How do we credit AI without pretending it did everything?"

## Build Block (1:00 - 1:50)

### Project: Mini-Showcase Package

Create a short package with:

- Best prompt recipe from Day 2
- Creative sample from Day 3
- One example of an AI mistake or limitation
- Responsible-use reflection:
  - What AI helped with
  - What you changed yourself
  - What you would verify before publishing

**Stretch:** Add a "rules I will follow when using AI" checklist.

## Play Block (2:00 - 2:40)

### Activity: Myth Buster Challenge

Teams respond to claims like:

- "AI always tells the truth."
- "Using AI means you did no work."
- "AI can replace learning."
- "If an AI says it confidently, it must be right."

Teams get XP for clear explanations and strong examples.
